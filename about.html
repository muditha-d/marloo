Origin of the Name Marloo

Marloo is a term from several eastern Australian Indigenous languages, like Warlpiri 'marlu,' referring specifically to kangaroos, especially the red kangaroo.

This name honors the deep connection to Australian Indigenous culture and the land's original custodians.

Stan Yarramanua - June 2025

A MAN CALLED “YARRA”

Stan's personal story is one of resilience, cultural reconnection, and transformation; raised in poverty in Swan Hill and often on the move with his itinerant father, Stan experienced many of the same social, economic, and emotional challenges that continue to impact Aboriginal communities today; like many young Aboriginal men, he faced systemic disadvantage, early exposure to substance abuse, and encounters with the justice system; however, Stan's turning point came through the healing power of cultural identity and art.

By embracing his heritage and artistic talent, Stan gave up alcohol and found purpose through painting and crafting traditional instruments like clapsticks, didgeridoos, and boomerangs; this cultural awakening led to the opening of one of Australia's first privately owned Aboriginal art galleries, where he promoted Indigenous artists from across the country, including remote desert regions; as a cultural ambassador, Yarra has shared his story and heritage internationally, but his deepest focus remains local—uplifting his people and creating lasting opportunities for future generations.

Social Responsibility

Marloo Indigenous Employment Pathways (MEIP)

The Marloo Indigenous Employment Pathways (MIEP) program is wholly funded by Marloo Solutions and is designed to support Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander people of all ages in gaining and maintaining employment in the field of their choice.

Each participant is guided by a dedicated mentor who provides tailored, culturally sensitive, practical, and emotional support throughout their employment journey.

Our support doesn't end at job placement — we walk alongside each participant for as long as needed, helping them build confidence, resilience, and experience to thrive, whether employed by Marloo or elsewhere, with every pathway tailored to the individual to maximise long-term outcomes and foster independence in the workforce.
