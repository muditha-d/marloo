Did You Know?

Trivia:
The name Australia derives from the Latin word ‘australis’, meaning “southern.” It dates back to 2nd century legends of an “unknown southern land” known as Terra Australis Incognita.

Explorer Matthew Flinders later named the continent Terra Australis, which was eventually abbreviated to the name we know today—Australia.
